A Little About Myself

Hi, I'm Sim! As a graduate from the University of British Columbia (UBC) with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Statistics, I am excited to bring my technical skills and academic experience to a new role in software development or data science.

I have developed a strong foundation in software engineering principles and programming languages such as Java, Python, and Typescript. I have also gained a proficiency for data analysis tools like R and SQL. Throughout my coursework, I worked on several projects, both independently and in groups, where I gained valuable experience in designing, implementing, and testing software systems as well as data cleansing, data wrangling, data visualization, and developing machine learning models.

During my time at UBC, I had the opportunity to work as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for CPSC 121 and CPSC 203 and an Undergraduate Academic Assistant for CPSC 203, where I developed my communication and leadership skills while helping students learn programming fundamentals and develop their problem-solving abilities. In these roles, I gained a deep understanding of how to design effective and engaging course materials, and how to provide constructive feedback to help students grow and improve.

In addition to my academic experience, I participated in an internship as a Junior Software Engineer. I gained hands-on experience in developing Java backend applications and enhancing existing software systems. I worked with REST/SOAP APIs, JUnit tests, and agile development methodologies to ensure the software was robust and scalable. I also honed my skills in agile development methodologies and version control systems like Git, working closely with other developers to manage project timelines, prioritize tasks, and ensure that code changes were properly tracked and documented.

I am eager to apply my academic and professional experience. If you are looking for a driven and dedicated person with a strong academic background and professional experience, I would love to connect and see how I can contribute to your team.
